  Steve Friedman spent decades in corporate America wearing a mask — performing extroversion every day until it cost him his energy, his health, and his sense of self. He's the author of *The Corporate Introvert: How to Lead and Thrive with Confidence*, and he's built a five-phase framework — Unaware, Uninformed, Enlightened, Content, Flourishing — that maps the exact journey from pretending to be someone else to succeeding as exactly who you are. His practical systems include the Ready-Set-Go-Next meeting framework and a Networking Intimacy approach designed for people who'd rather have one real conversation than work a room of a hundred strangers.
  
  Steve's work is for introverts who want to advance — not by fixing themselves, but by building on what already makes them effective. His entire approach is grounded in the idea that your quiet strengths aren't a liability to manage; they're the competitive advantage you've been underselling.
