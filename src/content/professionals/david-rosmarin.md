  Dr. David Rosmarin is a psychologist at McLean Hospital and Harvard Medical School who has spent 25 years studying why some people thrive under anxiety — and others don't. His research-backed framework flips the script: anxiety isn't a sign something is wrong with you, it's a signal you care deeply enough to be there. His book *Thriving with Anxiety* gives leaders nine concrete tools to stop fighting their inner experience and start working with it.
  
  For introverts, who often carry a private weight of self-doubt and shame without anyone knowing, David's work is unusually relevant. His most powerful finding — that having just one person you can be genuinely real with is the single strongest predictor of thriving — cuts straight to the isolation that holds so many quiet leaders back.
