## Key Stories
- **The Ed Sheeran creativity story**: Susanna shares a clip of Ed Sheeran saying he couldn't sing or play guitar at 14 — talent is cultivated through hours of practice, not born. Applied to introverts: your depth of thinking is practiced, not accidental.
- **Training herself to be creative 9–5**: As a designer, Susanna had to train her brain to produce creative work on demand rather than waiting for inspiration — a discipline that applies directly to introverted knowledge workers.
- **Sleep on it as leadership practice**: She actively advises clients to sleep between sessions, finding that the subconscious produces better solutions than real-time pressure.

## Techniques & Frameworks
- **Shrink-wrap your expertise**: Distill what you know into a simple, repeatable visual framework that communicates your authority without you having to be present.
- **SPARK approach**: Susanna's method for building authority that compounds over time through consistent intellectual property.
- **Sleep on it**: Deliberately delaying decisions and solutions to let the subconscious process — framed as a leadership tool, not a productivity failure.
- **Don't outsource your creativity to AI**: AI can refine and condense, but the deep original thinking — the introvert's edge — can't be replaced.
