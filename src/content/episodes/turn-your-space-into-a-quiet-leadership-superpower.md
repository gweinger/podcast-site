## Key Stories
- **The Woman with Depression**: A client who said "I don't want to wake up in the morning" had her bedroom and office in the wrong rooms for her personal energy number (water), with art of a hunched-over woman above her bed. After swapping the rooms and removing the artwork, she stopped taking depression and anxiety medication within two weeks, joined a club, started hobbies including improv, and eventually purchased three business franchises.
- **The Corporate Executive Who Couldn't Sleep**: A 50-year-old executive who had "never had a good night's sleep" in 15 years in her apartment switched her bedroom and office based on a Feng Shui chart for her building. The next day she slept through the night for the first time. Over two years she paid off $100,000 in student debt, received recognition in high-visibility corporate events, got engaged, and began building a new life.
- **The Job Seeker in One Room**: A young woman who had applied for jobs every day for seven months with no results; after Feng Shui adjustments to just her desk orientation and element placement in her single room, she signed a contract seven days later.

## Techniques & Frameworks
- **Personal Element System**: Based on your birth date, you have a personal energy element (fire, earth, metal, water, or wood). Understanding your element helps you identify what's missing or overabundant in your space and correct it with targeted additions.
- **Three R Framework**: Reframe (get clear on what you actually want and why), Rearrange (Feng Shui adjustments to your environment), and Reinvigorate (take aligned action—the space creates conditions but you still have to show up).
- **Attention with Intention**: The operating principle—every change to your space should be made with a clear intention for your life, not just aesthetic preference.
- **The Subconscious Speaks in Images**: Your surroundings speak directly to your subconscious. Artwork, photos, and objects that don't reflect who you want to become are actively working against you.
- **Acupuncture for Space**: Feng Shui is described as adjusting the chi flow in a building the way acupuncture adjusts chi flow in the body—targeted, energetic, and cumulative.
